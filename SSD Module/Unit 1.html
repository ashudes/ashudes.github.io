What?

The first unit introduced the contrasting management approaches to software development, highlighting the shift from traditional waterfall methods to agile methodologies. So What?

The transition from waterfall to agile reflects the industry's move towards more flexible and responsive development cycles, which can better accommodate the need for security. However, the flexibility of agile method should compromise on the security aspects.

Reflections

Software and Data Integrity

Introduction

Software and data integrity is the assurance that data remains accurate, complete, and consistent throughout its lifecycle. The Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) triad is a popular model considered as bedrock for information security architecture, providing a framework for ensuring the protection of data in various states (Pillai, 2017; Chandramouli et al., 2024). 

Software and Data Integrity, a key component of the CIA triad, refers to the protection of data and software against unauthorised alterations or destructions, ensuring that information remains authentic and trustworthy. It ensures that the data is unchanged from its source and has not been tampered with during storage, retrieval, or processing (Chandramouli et al., 2024). 

OWASP 2021 ranked Software and Data Integrity at number 8 and mentions a common example of how a breach in software and data integrity can be seen in an insecure Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD) pipeline. Without adequate security measures, applications that rely on plugins, libraries, or components from untrusted sources can introduce malicious codes and compromise the software and data integrity (OWASP, 2021).

Importance of Software and Data Integrity

The advent of CI/CD has revolutionized the way we develop and maintain software. It has increased the speed of software delivery and has become a popular choice among organizations for its efficiency (Pauline et al 2019). Bird 2016 compares this effect of software delivery as similar to the with way Agile and Test-Driven Development (TDD) (Bird, 2016). 

However, this popularity has also made it a target for malicious attacks if not secured properly. A notable example of a breach due to software integrity failure is the Equifax data breach in 2017. According to the report from Kabanov and Madnick (2020), the company suffered significant financial losses amounting to $17.5 Billion due to vulnerabilities in the Apache Struts framework, highlighting the importance of maintaining software and data integrity.

Mitigation Measures

From my readings, here are some of the strategies to prevent software and data integrity failures. 

  • Use digital signatures to ensure software or data is from a trusted source.
  • Regularly update pipeline configurations
  • Use software supply chain security tool to verify that components do not contain vulnerabilities.
  • Put in place a process to review pipeline configuration changes

(OWASP, 2021; Pan et al., 2023).

Conclusion

Software and Data Integrity is a crucial aspect of information security. It ensures that data remains accurate, consistent, and reliable throughout its lifecycle. By understanding its importance and implementing preventive measures, organizations can protect themselves against potential breaches and maintain the trust of their stakeholders.
